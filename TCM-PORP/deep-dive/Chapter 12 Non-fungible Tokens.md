# Non-fungible tokens
Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are unique blockchain tokens that can represent art, credentials, game items, memberships, or other assets. The token is unique, but the linked media can still be copied. NFT ownership does not automatically transfer copyright or broader legal rights. Smart contracts define token functions such as minting, transfers, royalties, collection logic, and metadata links. Licences, marketplace terms, and law determine permitted use.

Control usually follows the wallet private key. If an attacker steals that key and transfers the token, the blockchain records the attacker-controlled wallet as holder. That record does not necessarily settle legal ownership.
## Common NFT crimes
NFT crime exploits unclear valuation, hype, and weak wallet security.

- Ponzi schemes pay earlier investors with new investor funds.
- Rug pulls attract buyers, then developers abandon the project, sell holdings, or drain funds.
- Fake or low-value NFTs use promotion, impersonation, wash trading, or celebrity endorsement to create artificial demand.
- Get-rich-quick scams overstate rarity, utility, or future value.
- Phishing imitates marketplaces, exchanges, or wallets to steal credentials, seed phrases, or transaction approvals.
## Analysis methods
NFT analysis starts with wallet addresses, token IDs, contract addresses, marketplace profiles, and images. Marketplaces can expose collections, sales, profile names, social links, and activity. Blockchain explorers can show balances, transactions, contracts, and cross-chain use. Polygon now uses POL as the native gas and staking token on Polygon PoS.

Analysts compare wallets across marketplaces, search usernames, check ENS names, and review social profiles. ENS maps readable names such as example.eth to blockchain identifiers and may reveal registrant, controller, or linked address data. Reverse image search and NFT-specific search can uncover reused artwork, copied projects, artist sites, and hidden links.

Metadata may point to IPFS, Arweave, or centralised storage. It can include token IDs, contract addresses, traits, media links, standards, and blockchain details. Smart contract details help verify provenance, detect suspicious minting, and trace transfers.
